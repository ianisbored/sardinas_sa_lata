## Quilantang, 2024
- The Public Utility Vehicle Modernization Program (PUVMP) was introduced by the Department of Transportation (DOTr) in the Philippines back in 2017. Its primary objective is to replace conventional jeepneys with safer, more comfortable, and eco-friendly alternatives (Talabong). However, while this move towards modernisation holds the promise of a more streamlined and effective public transportation system, it also brings about questions regarding the future of these iconic vehicles as jeepneys are deeply embedded in the Filipino automotive culture and landscape.
- The Department of Transportation (DOTr) of the Philippines launched the Public Utility Vehicle Modernization Program (PUVMP) in 2017, the most extensive transportation reform initiative by the government to date (Talabong). The programme aims to replace old, dilapidated jeepneys with safer, more comfortable, and environmentally-friendly vehicles. Although the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic put this programme on hold temporarily, the government resumed its push for the modernisation programme in 2023 (The Manila Bulletin Team).
- The PUVMP, while promoting efficiency, safety, and environmental conservation, also raises questions regarding the potential cultural implications of this overhaul. The iconic jeepney, a vibrant symbol of Filipino resilience, ingenuity, and identity, is at risk of gradually disappearing from urban landscapes, supplanted by uniform, standardised vehicles (Suarez 204).
- Its unique blend of creativity and historical legacy symbolises the Filipino spirit of innovation, adaptation, and community. The potential erasure of this symbol and the icons enshrined with it from urban landscapes could impact the richness and diversity of Filipino culture not just indigenously but also on the global stage (Meñez 42).
- While the move towards modernisation and sustainability in the transportation sector is necessary and holds numerous advantages, the potential cultural implications of this shift require careful consideration. Progress must not inadvertently lead to the erasure of cultural identity and heritage. As the nation moves forward, preserving cultural symbols and the rich narratives they encompass should also be a concern.
- However, it has yet to delve into the modernisation programme’s broader societal, economic, and environmental implications. In response, the government, in its modernisation efforts, could engage with local artists, designers, and cultural practitioners to develop standardised designs for the new vehicles that incorporate and re-imagine traditional jeepney art. They could also engage with jeepney drivers and operators, passengers, and community members to gain insights into the cultural symbols that are most relevant and significant to them. This bottom-up approach to design would ensure that the new vehicles are more efficient and safer and continue to reflect Filipino culture and identity.
- Additionally, a system could be established to allow drivers or operators to personalise a portion of their vehicles, providing a canvas for individual expression, much like traditional jeepneys. This would serve as a testament to the resilience and creativity of the Filipino people, reinforcing national identity amidst the backdrop of modernity.
- Moreover, developing a national database or museum dedicated to preserving and displaying jeepney art could be another viable solution. This would allow future generations to appreciate jeepney art’s cultural richness and significance, even as the country moves towards modernisation.
